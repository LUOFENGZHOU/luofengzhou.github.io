Are Short Sellers Informed? Evidence from the Short-selling Activities before Stock Trading Halts. [PDF]
Ting Hu and Luofeng Zhou, forthcoming in Journal of Financial Research (Chinese top 3 journal in finance).

Stay Distinct, Stay Silent: The Impact of Business Strategy on Disclosure Choice. [PDF]
Rajiv D. Banker, Wei Shi and Luofeng Zhou.

Using validated textual measures based on earnings conference calls and 10-Ks, this paper provides novel evidence on how firms make disclosure choices given their business strategies. Although analysts tend to ask more product-related questions to innovative firms during calls, these firms successfully protect their secrets via more non-answers to proprietary-related questions, gaining competitive advantages and then capitalizing them into their firm-values.

Meta-Embeddings Based on Self-Attention. [PDF]
Qichen Li, Yuanqing Lin, Luofeng Zhou and Jian Li*, under review.

In this paper, we devise a new meta-embedding model based on the self-attention mechanism, namely Duo, which achieves state-of-the-art accuracy in text classification tasks. Based on Duo, we then propose a novel meta-embedding sequence-to-sequence model for machine translation tasks, with high convergence speed.

DMD: Discovering Time-Variant Temporal Dependence from Time Series Using Predictive Codelength. [PDF][CODE]
Luofeng Zhou, Zixuan Cao and Bo Han*, under review.

Using predictive codelength and sequential normalized maximum likelihood estimators, we propose a novel model, DMD, to identify time-variant temporal dependence from the perspective of changes in certainty.
